I'm a bioinformatician working in the field of Gene Annotation - with a special focus on non-protein coding RNA genes. I have a diploma and a PhD in Computer Science both concentrating on non-protein coding RNA genes. I developed the tools RNAmicro, SnoReport, GotohScan, and most recently ePoPE. While RNAmicro and SnoReport are programs that predict novel miRNAs and snoRNAs, resp. using the Support Vector Machine as their machine learning approach; GotohScan implements an efficient variant of Gotoh's semi-global alignment algorithm [Gotoh(1982)]. ePoPE is a dynamic programming approach to identify the last common ancestor and the history of the evolution of paralogs of a set of related genes given a phylogenetic tree.

Currently, I work as a post-doctoral researcher in the Bioinformatics Group of Peter Stadler at the Department of Computer Sciene of the University of Leipzig, Germany. I am giving lectures, seminars and lab courses; and supervise a number of master and PhD students.

In my spare time I do some data analytics in dental medicine, develop web pages and databases. I further develop some artwork for advertising purpose - like impressive flyers, katalogs, accessoires using Adobe's Photoshop and Corel's Graphic Suite.

I am a mother of two kids and together with my husband I live in a house in the countryside east of Leipzig. We enjoy the nature and do a lot of sports together.
