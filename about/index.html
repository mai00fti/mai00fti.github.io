I'm a bioinformatician working the field of Gene Annotation - with a special focus on non-protein coding RNA genes. I have a diploma and a PhD in Computer Science both concentrating on non-protein coding RNA genes. I developed the tools [RNAmicro](http://www.tbi.univie.ac.at/~jana/software/RNAmicro.html), [SnoReport](http://www.bioinf.uni-leipzig.de/Software/snoReport/), [GotohScan](http://www.bioinf.uni-leipzig.de/Software/GotohScan/GotohScan_2.0-alpha.tar.gz), and most recently [ePoPE](http://www.bioinf.uni-leipzig.de/Software/ePoPE/). While RNAmicro and SnoReport are programs that predict novel miRNAs and snoRNAs, resp. using the Support Vector Machine as their machine learning approach; GotohScan implements an efficient variant of Gotoh's semi-global alignment algorithm [Gotoh(1982)](http://jaligner.sourceforge.net/references/gotoh1982.pdf).

Now I work as a post-doctoral researcher in the Bioinformatics Group of Peter Stadler at the Department of Computer Sciene of the University of Leipzig, Germany. I am giving lectures, seminars and lab courses; and supervise a number of master and PhD students.
